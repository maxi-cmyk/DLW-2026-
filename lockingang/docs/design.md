# Design Principles & Guidelines

### 1. Constraint over Freedom

The Rule of 3 prevents choice paralysis. Limitations are features.

### 2. Active over Passive

Every interaction drives active recall. Re-reading is replaced by quizzes, and progress requires demonstrated understanding.

### 3. Visual over Textual

The Fog of War overlay, forgetting forecasts, and color-coded nodes make memory decay tangible and intuitive rather than abstract.

### 4. Compassion over Shame

Bridge nodes and grandparent resets treat failure as structural data points to be solved — not personal flaws. The system adapts the curriculum to the student, not the other way around.

### 5. Explainable over Opaque

Every recommendation comes with reasoning. The student always understands why the system is suggesting a particular action, building trust and metacognitive awareness.

### 6. Adaptive over Static

The system continuously evolves its model of the student. Long gaps, bursts of activity, and shifting priorities are all handled gracefully by the decay model and scheduling optimizer.
